Who We Are

About Faithful Paths CBO

Faithful Paths is a registered Community Based Organization in Kenya committed to creating positive change in the lives of women and youth. We believe in the power of community-driven solutions and the potential within every individual.

Empowerment

We provide tools and opportunities for self-reliance and growth.

Hope

We inspire confidence and optimism for a better future.

Inclusivity

We welcome and support all members of our community.

Service

We are dedicated to serving our community with integrity.

Our Work

What We Do

Women Empowerment Programs

  • Skills training in tailoring, cooking, and crafts
  • Entrepreneurship and business development
  • Financial literacy and savings groups
  • Leadership development workshops
  • Microfinance and startup support

Youth Development Programs

  • Mentorship and career guidance
  • Education support and scholarships
  • Digital skills and computer training
  • Sports and recreational activities
  • Life skills and personal development

Community Services

  • Health awareness and outreach programs
  • Environmental conservation initiatives
  • Family support and counseling services
  • Community clean-up campaigns
  • Emergency response and disaster relief

Counseling & Mentorship

  • Individual and group counseling sessions
  • Peer support networks
  • Mental health awareness programs
  • Trauma counseling and healing
  • Spiritual guidance and support

Stories of Hope & Impact

Mary's Transformation

"Through Faithful Paths' tailoring program, I learned valuable skills that helped me start my own business. Today, I employ three other women and support my family independently."

- Mary Wanjiku, Women Empowerment Graduate

John's Journey

"The mentorship program changed my life. I received guidance on career choices and now I'm pursuing my dream of becoming a software developer."

- John Ochieng, Youth Program Participant

Community Health Impact

"Our village health has improved significantly since Faithful Paths started the health awareness programs. We now have better sanitation and disease prevention practices."

- Grace Muthoni, Community Leader
